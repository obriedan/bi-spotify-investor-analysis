2. Single Most Pressing Challenge

Pricing Power vs. Subscriber Retention Trade-off

Spotify announced its third price increase on January 15, 2026, raising U.S. Individual plans from $11.99 to $12.99/month.2 This widens the gap vs. Apple Music ($10.99) to $2/month. JPMorgan analysts estimate the increase could add approximately $500M in revenue,3 but the timing is precarious: SPOT stock has fallen 44% from its June 2025 peak of $785 to approximately $440,4 suggesting the market is already pricing in subscriber churn risk. The Q4 2025 earnings (due February 10, 2026) will be the critical test — any sign of subscriber growth deceleration will validate bearish concerns about pricing elasticity.

This is a NOW issue, not a perennial concern: the January 2026 price increase is the first since mid-2024, it makes Spotify the most expensive mainstream music streaming service, and the stock reaction has been severe.

3. Management Actions for the Next 90 Days

  • Product differentiation acceleration: Launched AI "Prompted Playlists" in US/Canada (January 22, 2026)5 and "About the Song" feature (February 6, 2026)6 to justify premium pricing with features competitors lack.
  • Content expansion: Physical book sales via Bookshop.org and Netflix podcast partnership (January 2026) to expand TAM beyond pure audio streaming.
  • Q4 2025 earnings release (February 10, 2026): Guidance targets 289M Premium subscribers (+8M QoQ), €4.5B revenue, and 32.9% gross margin — beating these would reassure investors on the profitability thesis.
